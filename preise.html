Self-diagnostic

Is your organisation facing symptoms — or causality?

If several of these land uncomfortably, it usually signals the system — not the people — is constraining performance.

01Competing in a highly contested industry ecosystemMargins are pressured from several directions at once.
02The business model no longer serves the organisation, its people, or shareholdersWhat once worked now produces strain instead of returns.
03High turnover but threatened profitabilityActivity is high while the financial trajectory is falling.
04The model needs innovation to stay competitiveIncremental tweaks are no longer enough to keep pace.
05Scalability of the current operating model is in doubtGrowth adds cost and friction faster than it adds value.
06The organisation has moved from growth/maturity into declineThe curve has turned and momentum is working against you.
07Senior leaders are under sustained stressPressure is constant, and the bandwidth to think clearly is shrinking.
08Fear is present in the organisationPeople protect themselves rather than the enterprise.
09Competitors are circlingDominant incumbents and niche challengers are both applying pressure.
10Repeated failed attempts to shift the organisationInitiatives launch with energy and quietly revert to the old pattern.
11Latent conflict is blocking progressUnspoken tension stalls decisions and execution.
12Considering cosmetic changes to surviveCost cutting, asset sales, or financial engineering are on the table.

If several of these resonate

It is rarely a people problem. It is usually the system. The next step is a confidential conversation to frame the challenge in causality terms and agree on the few leverage points that matter.
